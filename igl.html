What's going on?
The ideal gas law is an equation that shows the relationship of the properties of a gas in a sealed container. The equation is PV = nRT, where P is pressure, V is volume, n is moles, R is the constant 0.0821, and T is temperature.

In this simulation, we have made V and n constant. This is therefore simulating a sealed metal container that can withstand high pressure, and we do not add or remove any matter. To interact with the simulation, move the sliders to see the relationship between temperature and pressure under these conditions.
Please use a computer and make the window full width in order to see the simulation.
